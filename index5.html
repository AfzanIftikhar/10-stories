The God Of Small Things

"The God of Small Things" is a novel written by Arundhati Roy, published in 1997. Set in Kerala, India, the novel
explores themes of social norms, love, family dynamics, and the impact of caste and societal expectations on
individuals.

Summary of the story

1: Introduction to the Setting:

The novel is set in the village of Ayemenem in Kerala, India, during the 1960s. It revolves around two main
characters, Estha and Rahel, who are twins

2: Family Background:

Estha and Rahel live with their mother, Ammu, and their maternal grandparents, Mammachi and Pappachi.
Ammu is divorced from their father, who lives abroad.

3: Visitors to Ayemenem:

The family's lives are disrupted by the arrival of Sophie Mol, the daughter of their English cousin, and her mother,
Margaret Kochamma. Sophie Mol's presence affects the dynamics between the family members and the twins.

4: Social Hierarchies and Taboos:

The novel delves into the rigid social hierarchies and caste prejudices prevalent in Kerala. Ammu, who falls in love with Velutha, a lower-caste man who works for the family, faces severe societal backlash.

5: Forbidden Love:

Ammu and Velutha's love is forbidden due to the caste differences. When their relationship is discovered, Velutha is brutally beaten by the police, and Ammu is ostracized by her family.

6: Tragedy Strikes:

Tragedy strikes when Sophie Mol drowns in the river under mysterious circumstances. The incident deeply affects the lives of everyone in the family and the village.

7: Flashbacks and Present Day:

The narrative alternates between the events leading up to Sophie Mol's death and the twins' lives as adults returning to Ayemenem years later.

THEME:

Love and Loss: The novel explores different forms of love and the profound impact of loss on individuals and families. Social Injustice: It critiques the injustices of the caste system and societal norms that restrict personal freedoms and perpetuate inequality. Memory and Trauma: The narrative is woven with memories and trauma that shape the characters' identities and relationships. Forbidden Desires: It examines the consequences of pursuing desires that defy social conventions and norms. Family and Betrayal: The novel portrays the complexities of familial relationships, including loyalty, betrayal, and the burden of family expectations. "The God of Small Things" received critical acclaim for its lyrical prose, intricate narrative structure, and powerful exploration of themes. It won the Booker Prize in 1997, making Arundhati Roy the first Indian woman to receive this prestigious literary award.
